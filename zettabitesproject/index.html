As all of us know, when we approach the southern part of the country, Temperature gets higher. If we go even lower, into central America, we will find the temperature to be even higher. Is there a pattern in temperature? Here is some data:

As you can see, this is a scatter plot, relating the latitudinal location of U.S. cities, such as Atlanta or Los Angeles, to the relative temperature exhibited in each city.

It seems to be that the lower a city in the US, the higher the temperature there. But because it doesn't include specific location, we charted a graph that combined the location, latitude + longitude, and the temperature displayed. We did this, and got the following:

The not-so-surprising-discovery:

After we had the data, it was obvious we had to interpret what we were seeing, so we did. What we saw was that, indeed, cities nearer to the equator are hotter, and further north are not as hot.
