Overview
This project measures the significant aspects of health concerns around the world. Relating to every populated continent, the data statistics present information of apparent concern. These health-related issues include that of blood lead content, second hand smoke, and life expectancies per populated continent.


Why we chose this project

As a group we chose this specific project because of growing concern for people's health around the world. The impact of which outside forces have made on people is drastic. Thus, the following data is presented in order to show the surprising correlation between nations.


Key Insight

The graphs represented show the health data around the globe. The correlation among the WHO regions of health crises include blood lead levels, secondhand smoke, and life expectancy. In the three data sets, the blood lead data set shows the highest lead in countries in which they are developing, but have a high density of people per capita. In the second hand smoke data set, the data shows the highest smoke content in developed countries, but with not much restriction on smoking. In the life expectancy data set, the data shows the lowest life expectancy in western nations.

What's important

The most important data in each of the data sets is the region that is most affected by each of the health occurrences and the number of cases that occur in percentages of content and in years.

Why does it matter

This data compiled from each shows the concern the world should have for their health, and shows why the world invests so much on healthcare (over $600 Billion).
