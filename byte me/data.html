Data

Our data proves that the majority of mass shooters tend to be white males (96%), while the minority races comprise only a small percentage of all perpetrators. On the other hand, mass shootings committed by females, or committed jointly by a male and a female are extremely rare (4%).
Statistics show that over 50% of shooters suffer from mental illness prior to the act of violence, which is in accordance with conventional stereotypes. The mental state of some shooters is unable to be determined either because of death or suicide at the shooting, or a lack of evidence to support a conclusive diagnosis.
Barring the miscellaneous venues, it is evident that the most common places for a mass shooting to occur is in the workplace and schools. This data could be used to prevent future attacks and increase safety measures in high risk areas.
In the chart on the left, the data displays the number of victims wounded or killed in mass shootings by year. It is evident that the highest number of deaths and wounded by mass shooting can be seen in the year 2012. In the chart on the right, the amount of individuals killed and wounded are combined to show a broader scope of the data. It can be interpreted from this graph that the last four years have generated more mass shootings than any other four year period in history.
