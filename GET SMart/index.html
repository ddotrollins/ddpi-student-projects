Who Has Water?

by GET SMart

Not everyone in the world has access to clean drinkable water, like we have in the US. Throughout the world there are people that do not use improved drinking water sources. Some countries have worse statistics than others, but it’s clear to see the global disparities. We chose to represent this particular set of data because we believe that everyone has the right to drink clean water and it’s important that even though we have those resources we remember that elsewhere in the world some do not.

These graphs show the percent of the population using improved water sources in 2015, representing over 100 countries. Dozens of countries represented have less than 80% of their populations using improved drinking-water sources.

Over Time

This graph shows the increases or decreases in percentages over recent years, beginning with 1990 to 2015. Many countries have seen great advancements in their sanitation and water access but this progress is not universal and countries still struggle, some have even seen drops in percentages due to a variety of reasons, primarily natural disasters.

Rural v Urban

This graph depicts the compared percentages of the population using improved drinking-water in rural communities versus urban communities in particular countries in 2015. The countries were chosen based off their different data to prove that there are stark contrasts of the population drinking clean water in different communities. The countries selected were Chad, India, Pakistan, Swaziland, and Thailand. Chad had a 27% difference from the urban community compared to the rural. India had a 4.5% difference from the rural community to the urban. Pakistan had a 4% difference from urban to rural. Swaziland urban population had 24.7% more than the rural community. And finally, Thailand had a .4% greater rural population drinking clean water compared to the urban community.
